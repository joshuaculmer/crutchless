# About Me

My name is Joshua Culmer, I'm a Software Developer from Anaheim California who is passionate about optimization. Optimization drove me to learn about solving puzzles and code. Misoptimizating my life has led me to seriously contemplate how I spend my time and why. A big part of how I now optimize my life is by considering what brings me Joy and optimizing for that. I experience Joy through my family, the voice of God, and by being present.

# About the Crutch

My crutch is personal crutch is entertainment. By entertainment any application designed to consume its users attention. That definition is oddly specific but it neatly represents the habits that I am trying to break. Youtube, Facebook, Tik Tock, Memes, Scrolling, Video Games. These applications, and other modeled similarly, take much more mental real estate than I am willing to give them. When I indulge in this type of behavior, all concept of time goes away and even when I'm not actively engaged in the activity I find myself thinking about it. I have compulsively used entertainment as a coping mechanism for over a decade and I've been seriously trying to wean myself off it since Jan 2025.

# About Going Crutchless

Let me just preface by saying that despite my young cognitive plasticity, breaking a habit that has controlled my behavior for most of my life is hard. There were/are days when I just want that hit of dopamine and the cravings scream at me to open up a game or pull up just one Youtube video. Going crutchless sucks. But, not only do I believe that leaving behind my crutch will improve my life, I feel that it is a spiritual obligation, a commandment from God that I do so.

# About God

In April of 2022 I was preaching to the people of the Dominican Republic, walking down some dusty road when these words pierced my soul like a dagger: 'Remember the Gods you worshipped and how they treated you' Hundreds of miles away from home, my heart longed for the solice of playing a video game. And God told me to remember. To remember is the second most frequent commandment in the Bible, and is almost always accompanied by the most frequent commandment: Repent. In that moment, God helped me to remember the idols of metal and plastic. I remember spending hours building a Minecraft castle instead of completing homework. I remember staring at a phone screen till 3am watching Youtube instead of getting a fresh start to the next day. I remember those souless eyes staring back at me from a dark loading screen in a basement waiting for a game to load. The gods of entertainment made a slave of me and I remember how it felt. Remembering those days of slavery and expressing gratitude for the new life that God wants for me is a large contribution to the why of this blog.

# Crutchless, my Personal Blog

## Why make Crutchless into a Blog?

In part I write this blog to remember. In part I write this blog to share my journey with others who experience the same struggles and loss that I have. In part I write this blog to document my growth process and so others can hold me accountable. In part I write this blog because the endeavor of articulating my thoughts about this topic will help me remember, and if some soul is inspired by my writings, then it will have not only benefitted myself but others as well.

## What Crutchless is

I mostly plan to publish stream of consciousness writing about topics that relate to my experience going crutchless. I plan to discuss how I have changed my morning routines, how I use monthly metrics to track my habits, applicable insights for life from books I've read, and those types of things. I plan to update this blog weekly in an attempt to document my Joys and Struggles and plans and ideas for living crutchless. If reading through my journey is helpful to readers then all the better. That said...

## What Crutchless is not

This blog is not meant to be a self help guide or video game addiction therapy session. I am not a trained psychologist nor an expert in any way on how to fix your life. Heck, I am busy enough pinning down my own life's loose ends. If you need help, go get help. I am not liable for any life advice or reccomendations or any information that you, the reader, may find on this blog.
