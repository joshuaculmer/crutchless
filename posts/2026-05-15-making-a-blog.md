# I made a Blog

I really didn't see this one coming. I've given a lot of thought recently to how I live my life and working to change my habits. Atmoic Habits, 7 Habits of Highly Effective People, along with the New Testament and The Relentless Elimination of Hurry enjoy living in my head rent free. Working to kill the old habits, be more present, and do what I feel like God has been drilling into my thick skull to do has been a big priority since New Years.

## Metric Journal

I use journals as a means to both hold myself accountable and to express gratitude. This year I implemented a Habit Chart or Metric Journal as I like to call it. I make a table for each month, rows representing each day, columns representing each Metric. Come the end of the day as I wind down, I mark each day. And it has been wild to watch what happens.

As the days turn to months I discover that I have built a few new habits. Recording my Metrics for one, studying the scriptures, cleaning up the dishes, and overall building and maintaining my routines. Interestingly enough, the old habits die hard, much harder than the new ones form.

One of the metrics that I have been recording since I started in January is my screentime. The definition of screentime has changed, at first it was just screentime, then gaming, then high dopamine screentime in general. And despite seeing dramatic decrease on most days, there are some days where I almost can't help myself. Looking back at the past 4.5 months there is a pretty clear pattern, its 0 minutes most days, but every few days I have a rough day and have 2-4 hours of the exact habit that I am trying to kill.

Killing this habit has been incredibly frustrating, painful, and the least intuitive thing I have ever done. So in an attempt to document both my successes and failures, spend more time writing, and think about why I have decided to stop using my crutch of playing video games, consuming media, shorts, and memes, I have created this blog

## My Expectations for this blog

I would love to write a post here at least once a week, but honestly, we'll just see how it goes. Again, I'm writing for myself first and foremost, but if others find what I have to say useful, I will continue to write and document the journey. The act of writing and articulating what I want to say, my plans, hopes, dreams as I actively work to build a life that I find more satisfying is the true product that I hope to build. A blog is nice, sure. Sharing ideas with others is fantastic, but I'm no expert, just a random Joe that is working through life trying to figure out what works as best as I can. That said, I'm not really alone.

I've alluded a little bit to my relationship with God already here, which might sound a little odd in today's age where religion and spirituality aren't held in the highest esteem, but hear me out. God is a big part of my life, so I won't be divorcing Him from the part He plays on this epic of mine. In the [about](../about.html) page I talk about one of the driving phrases that the Holy Spirit has spoken directly to me, "Remember the Gods you worshiped and how they treated you".

That phrase has been burned indelibly into my soul as a reminder to forsake the ways of my youth and enjoy a higher and holier way of living through Christ. So, yes I will discuss my spirituallity as part of my journey. My record would be incomplete without it. But more than just focusing on Christ and how He teaches me, this blog is focused on my attempts to improve my life, optimize for Joy, and hopefully share those ideas with other who might be interested.
